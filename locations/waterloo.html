Scratched, Dull, and Stained — But Not Beyond Saving

Hardwood floors that look like they need to be replaced usually don't. Refinishing removes years of wear by sanding to bare wood and building a fresh finish from scratch — what comes back looks like new installation and costs a fraction of replacement. Twin Lakes homes, including seasonal cottages and year-round residences, often have great hardwood under worn finishes. The free estimate answers the key question: can your floors be saved, and what will it actually cost?

What Waiting Is Doing to Your Floors

  • Scratches go deeper over time. Once the finish layer is broken, bare wood absorbs grit and moisture. Surface damage doesn't stay on the surface.
  • Water damage is cumulative. Repeated moisture events penetrate further into the wood each season — there's a point where refinishing stops being possible.
  • Pre-sale window is short. Floor refinishing requires cure time. If you're listing, schedule this before the rush.
  • Replacement costs 3–4x more. Refinishing at $3.50–$7/sqft vs. new hardwood at $6–$12/sqft. Good wood is almost always worth refinishing.

How We Refinish Floors in Waterloo

1. Free in-home estimate. Written quote — full refinish, screen-and-recoat, or board replacement.

2. Stain samples in your home. Test patches on your actual floor, in your actual light. You approve before we commit.

3. Dustless sanding. Containment-and-vacuum system captures dust at the source.

4. Finish coats. Two to three coats of commercial-grade polyurethane, buffed between each.

5. Cure and handoff. Written protocol before we leave — walk-on times and furniture return schedule.

Quick Answers Before You Call

Will there be a lot of dust?

Not with our dustless sanding system — it captures the vast majority of dust at the source.

What if I choose the wrong stain color?

You choose from test patches on your actual floor. You approve it before we proceed.

How long until I can use the room?

Water-based: foot traffic in 24 hours, furniture in 72. Oil-based: 48 hours for foot traffic, 7 days for furniture.
