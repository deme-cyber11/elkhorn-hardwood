Hardwood Floor Refinishing in Chalco

One of Elkhorn' most established neighborhoods deserves hardwood floors restored to match its stunning mountain views.

Chalco's Established Homes & Classic Floors

Chalco sits on the northwest side of Elkhorn, nestled against the foothills with panoramic views of Omaha metro and the Front Range. Developed primarily in the 1970s through 1990s, this established neighborhood features a mix of split-level, ranch, and two-story homes — many with original hardwood flooring that's been the foundation of family life for 30–50 years.

The homes in Chalco were built during an era when solid 3/4-inch red oak and white oak were standard in main living areas. These are excellent floors — thick, well-milled, and installed over solid subfloors. But decades of mountain living have taken their toll: UV fading from Chalco's spectacular west-facing windows, wear from generations of family foot traffic, and the dry-climate gapping that every Elkhorn homeowner knows too well.

Common Flooring Challenges in Chalco Homes

  • Severe UV sun damage: Chalco's prized west-facing mountain views come with a cost — intense afternoon sun that fades hardwood stain dramatically. Rooms with large windows often show stark color differences between exposed and furniture-covered areas.
  • Decades of accumulated wear: Thirty to fifty years of family life means deep wear patterns, especially in hallways, kitchen entries, and stairway landings that connect split-level floors.
  • Outdated dark or orange stains: 1970s and 1980s homes often have dated dark walnut or amber-toned stains that make rooms feel smaller. A modern stain color update opens these spaces dramatically.
  • Split-level stair refinishing: Chalco's many split-level homes mean stair treads and risers that need refinishing in coordination with each floor level for color consistency.
