Scratched, Dull, and Stained — But Not Beyond Saving

Hardwood floors that look like they need to be replaced usually don't. Refinishing removes years of wear by sanding to bare wood and rebuilding the finish from scratch — what comes back looks like new installation and costs a fraction of replacement. Walworth County's cold winters stress finishes faster than in moderate climates, but underneath almost every worn floor is sound wood worth restoring. The free estimate tells you exactly what your floors need.

What Waiting Is Doing to Your Floors

  • Scratches go deeper over time. Once finish is broken, bare wood absorbs grit and moisture.
  • Water damage is cumulative. Penetrates deeper each season until refinishing is no longer an option.
  • Pre-sale window is short. Refinishing requires cure time — schedule before listing.
  • Replacement costs 3–4x more. Refinishing at $3.50–$7/sqft vs. new hardwood at $6–$12/sqft.

How We Refinish Floors in Walworth

1. Free in-home estimate — written quote, full refinish or screen-and-recoat.

2. Stain samples on your actual floor in your actual light. You approve before we commit.

3. Dustless sanding — captures dust at the source.

4. Three finish coats of commercial-grade polyurethane.

5. Written cure protocol before we leave.

Quick Answers Before You Call

Will there be a lot of dust?

Not with our dustless sanding system — captures dust at the source.

What if I choose the wrong stain?

You choose from test patches on your actual floor — before we proceed.

How long until I can use the room?

Water-based: 24 hours foot traffic, 72 hours furniture. Oil-based: 48 hours foot traffic, 7 days furniture.
