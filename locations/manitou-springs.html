This historic Victorian resort town at the foot of Pikes Peak is home to some of the region's most distinctive hardwood floors.

Manitou Springs' Eclectic Homes & Historic Floors

Manitou Springs is unlike any other community in the Pikes Peak region. Founded in the 1870s as a health resort around its natural mineral springs, this compact mountain town evolved into a vibrant artistic community with Victorian homes, eclectic cottages, and converted historic buildings lining its hilly streets along Fountain Creek canyon.

The hardwood floors in Manitou Springs homes tell the story of the town itself. Grand Victorian residences on Capitol Hill and Ruxton Avenue feature original longleaf pine and narrow-strip oak installed by craftsmen over a century ago. Mid-century cabins and cottages along the creek have Douglas fir and mixed species floors that have weathered decades of mountain humidity fluctuations.
