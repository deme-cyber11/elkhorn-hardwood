Scratched, Dull, and Stained — But Not Beyond Saving

Hardwood floors that look like they need to be replaced usually don't. The scratches, the worn patches near the doorways, the water ring that's been there since last spring — refinishing removes all of it by sanding down to fresh wood and building a new finish from scratch. What comes back looks like new installation, costs a fraction of replacement, and typically takes 2–3 days.

Burlington has a lot of older homes with original hardwood under decades of finish buildup. What looks like damage is usually just tired finish over perfectly good wood. The free estimate answers the real question: can your floors be saved, and what will it actually cost?

What Waiting Is Doing to Your Floors

  • Scratches go deeper over time. Once the finish layer is broken, bare wood absorbs grit, moisture, and oils from foot traffic. The damage that stops at the surface today doesn't stay there.
  • Water damage is cumulative. Repeated moisture events penetrate further into the wood each season. There's a point where refinishing stops being possible.
  • Pre-sale window is short. Floor refinishing requires cure time before furniture goes back. If you're planning to list, you need this done before the rush.
  • Replacement costs 3–4x more. Refinishing at $3.50–$7/sqft vs. new hardwood installation at $6–$12/sqft. If the wood underneath is good, refinishing is almost always the right call.

How We Refinish Floors in Valley

1. Free in-home estimate. We assess the wood — species, thickness, condition, depth of damage — and give you a written quote.

2. Stain samples in your home. You pick the color on your actual floor, in your actual light — not from a color card. You approve before we commit.

3. Dustless sanding. A containment-and-vacuum system captures dust at the source. Dramatically cleaner than traditional refinishing.

4. Stain, seal, and finish coats. Two to three coats of polyurethane, with buffing between each. Water-based options available for faster return to the space.

5. Cure and handoff. Light foot traffic in 24 hours (water-based) or 48 hours (oil-based). We give you the exact protocol for your finish before we leave.

Quick Answers Before You Call

Will there be a lot of dust?

Not with our dustless sanding system. It captures the vast majority of sanding dust at the source — dramatically different from traditional refinishing.

What if I choose the wrong stain color?

You don't choose from a card — you choose from test patches applied to your actual floor, in your actual light. You approve it before we proceed.

How long until I can use the room?

Water-based finish: foot traffic in 24 hours, furniture in 72. Oil-based: 48 hours for foot traffic, 7 days for furniture.
