Scratched, Dull, and Stained — But Not Beyond Saving

Hardwood floors that look like they need to be replaced usually don't. Refinishing removes years of wear by sanding to bare wood and building a fresh finish from scratch. Fontana homes on Geneva Lake face significant seasonal humidity swings — wet summers and very dry winters — which stress finishes faster than in moderate climates. But underneath almost every worn floor is sound wood that's worth restoring. The free estimate tells you exactly what your floors need and what it will actually cost.

What Waiting Is Doing to Your Floors

  • Scratches go deeper over time. Once finish is broken, bare wood absorbs grit and moisture. Surface damage escalates.
  • Water damage is cumulative. Penetrates deeper each season. Lakefront homes are especially exposed.
  • Pre-sale or rental prep window is short. Refinishing requires cure time — schedule before listing or before season opening.
  • Replacement costs 3–4x more. Refinishing at $3.50–$7/sqft. Good wood underneath is worth saving.

How We Refinish Floors in Fontana

1. Free in-home estimate — written quote, full refinish or screen-and-recoat. We assess wood species, thickness, and condition.

2. Stain samples on your actual floor in your actual light. You approve before we commit.

3. Dustless sanding — containment-and-vacuum captures dust at the source.

4. Three finish coats of commercial-grade polyurethane. We recommend water-based for lakefront homes — handles humidity variation better than oil-based.

5. Written cure protocol before we leave.

Quick Answers Before You Call

Will there be a lot of dust?

Not with our dustless sanding system — captures dust at the source, especially important in lake homes.

What if I choose the wrong stain?

You choose from test patches on your actual floor — before we proceed.

How long until I can use the room?

Water-based: 24 hours foot traffic, 72 hours furniture. Oil-based: 48 hours foot traffic, 7 days furniture.
