Hardwood Floor Services in Colorado Springs

Most Popular Service

Whole-House Sand & Refinish

Our most requested service transforms tired, scratched, and faded hardwood floors back to their original beauty ‚Äî or better. Whether you own a 1960s ranch in Rockrimmon with original red oak or a 2010s Briargate home with builder-grade flooring that's seen better days, whole-house refinishing is the most cost-effective way to dramatically improve your home's look and value.

Our Refinishing Process

Every project begins with a thorough floor assessment. We check board thickness with a moisture meter and gauge, identify problem areas like water damage or loose boards, and evaluate the existing finish. This ensures we choose the right sanding sequence and finish system for your specific floor.

  1. Preparation: We mask all baseboards, vents, and transitions. Doors are removed if needed. Nails are set below the surface to prevent sanding damage.
  2. Coarse Sanding: We start with 36-grit to remove the old finish and level the floor. This is where decades of wear, scratches, and stains disappear.
  3. Medium & Fine Sanding: Progressive passes with 60-grit and 100-grit create a smooth, uniform surface ready for stain or finish.
  4. Staining (Optional): If you're changing colors, we apply your chosen stain from our library of 40+ options. Test patches are always done first so you can approve the color in your home's lighting.
  5. Finish Application: Two to three coats of Bona Mega ONE waterborne polyurethane, with light buffing between coats. This commercial-grade finish is specifically rated for Colorado's UV intensity and low humidity.
  6. Final Inspection: Walk-through with you to ensure every square foot meets our standard. We provide a written care guide and schedule a 30-day follow-up check.

Premium Upgrade

Dustless Hardwood Floor Sanding

Traditional floor sanding generates massive amounts of fine wood dust that infiltrates every surface in your home ‚Äî cabinets, closets, HVAC ducts, electronics. At Colorado Springs' elevation of 6,035 feet, lower air density means fine particles stay airborne 40% longer than at sea level. For families with allergies, asthma, or simply anyone who doesn't want a week-long cleanup, dustless sanding is the clear choice.

How Our Dustless System Works

We use commercial-grade HEPA-filtered vacuum systems connected directly to every sanding tool via 4-inch hoses. The vacuums generate continuous negative pressure at the sanding point, capturing 99% of dust before it ever becomes airborne. The remaining 1%? Our HEPA air scrubbers handle that ‚Äî running continuously throughout the project to filter ambient air down to 0.3-micron particles.

Benefits for Colorado Springs Homes

  • No plastic sheeting needed ‚Äî save time and avoid the suffocating sealed-room approach
  • Stay in your home ‚Äî many families remain in the house during dustless projects, just avoiding the work area
  • Protect your HVAC ‚Äî at our altitude, furnaces run hard in winter; dust in your ducts means it recirculates for months
  • Better finish adhesion ‚Äî less ambient dust means fewer particles settling into wet finish coats, resulting in a smoother final surface
  • Pet and allergy safe ‚Äî critical for the many Colorado Springs households with pets and altitude-sensitive respiratory systems

Dustless Sanding Pricing

Dustless sanding adds $0.50‚Äì$1.00 per square foot over traditional sanding, bringing the total to approximately $3.50‚Äì$5.00 per square foot for a complete sand-and-refinish. For a 1,000 sq ft project, that's $500‚Äì$1,000 more ‚Äî a modest investment that eliminates the dust nightmare entirely. Most Colorado Springs homeowners who've experienced both methods say they'd never go back to traditional sanding.

Targeted Restoration

Hardwood Floor Repair & Board Replacement

Not every hardwood floor needs a full refinish. Sometimes the issue is localized ‚Äî water damage from a leaking dishwasher in your Briargate kitchen, pet stains that penetrated through the finish in a Rockrimmon living room, or a handful of cracked boards near the entry of your Old Colorado City bungalow. We handle targeted repairs that restore your floor without the cost and disruption of a whole-house project.

Common Repairs We Handle

  • Water damage restoration: Warped, cupped, or buckled boards from plumbing leaks, appliance failures, or flooding. We assess the subfloor, replace damaged boards, and refinish to blend seamlessly.
  • Board replacement: Individual boards that are cracked, split, or gouged beyond what sanding can fix. We carry a salvage inventory of reclaimed hardwood ‚Äî oak, maple, hickory, and walnut ‚Äî to match vintage floors in older Colorado Springs neighborhoods.
  • Gap filling: Colorado's dry winters cause hardwood to contract, leaving gaps between boards. We fill gaps with flexible wood filler that moves with seasonal expansion and contraction at our 30‚Äì40% humidity levels.
  • Pet damage repair: Deep scratches, urine stains that have penetrated the wood ‚Äî we sand to bare wood, treat with oxalic acid to bleach stains, and refinish to a uniform color.
  • Squeaky floor repair: Subfloor separation causing squeaks underfoot ‚Äî we refasten boards to joists and shim where needed.

Repair pricing ranges from $500‚Äì$2,000 per project depending on scope. Small repairs (under 20 sq ft) can often be completed in a single day. Send us photos of the damage for a quick estimate.

New Floors

Hardwood Floor Installation

Building a new home in Falcon or Monument? Replacing carpet with hardwood in your Security-Widefield ranch? We install solid and engineered hardwood flooring with the same precision and care we bring to refinishing. Proper installation starts with the subfloor ‚Äî and at Colorado Springs' altitude, acclimation and moisture management are absolutely critical.

Installation Methods

  • Nail-down installation: The traditional method for solid hardwood over wood subfloors. We use pneumatic nailers with the correct angle and spacing for each species. Best for 3/4-inch solid hardwood in homes with plywood or OSB subfloors.
  • Glue-down installation: Required for engineered hardwood over concrete ‚Äî common in Colorado Springs basements and slab-on-grade homes. We use moisture-barrier adhesives that prevent vapor transmission from concrete.
  • Floating installation: Engineered planks with click-lock profiles that float over an underlayment. Fastest method, ideal for budget-conscious projects or areas where subfloor conditions vary.

Why Acclimation Matters at 6,035 Feet

Wood shipped from mills at lower elevations arrives with higher moisture content than Colorado Springs' ambient conditions. If installed too soon, boards will shrink as they equilibrate to our 30‚Äì40% humidity, creating gaps and potential cupping. We require a minimum 7‚Äì10 day acclimation period with the wood stored in your home at normal living conditions. We verify moisture content with a pin meter before installation day ‚Äî your floor should read within 2% of the subfloor.

Species We Install

Red oak, white oak, hickory, maple, walnut, Brazilian cherry, and engineered options from Bona, Shaw, and Mohawk. We help you choose based on your lifestyle (kids? dogs?), traffic patterns, and aesthetic preferences. Hickory is increasingly popular in Colorado Springs for its hardness and rustic grain pattern that complements mountain-style homes.

Installation pricing: $6‚Äì$15 per square foot installed, including subfloor prep and transitions. Species, method, and layout complexity affect final pricing. Request a free installation estimate.
