Hardwood Floor Refinishing in Elkhorn, NE — Done Right, Guaranteed

Hardwood floor refinishing removes years of scratches, worn finish, and stain discoloration by sanding down to bare wood and building a fresh surface from scratch. Most Elkhorn homeowners need it when floors look dull, scuffed, or badly discolored — or before listing a home. Done correctly, refinished floors look better than the day they were installed. Done wrong or skipped too long, moisture damage can make refinishing impossible and full replacement unavoidable.

When You Need Hardwood Refinishing

The clearest warning sign is a finish that no longer repels water — water should bead on healthy hardwood, not soak in. Watch for deep scratches that cut through the finish into raw wood, gray or black staining around a water source (dishwasher, pet bowl, plant stand), floors that look dull even right after mopping, or worn color paths in high-traffic areas. Nebraska's cold, dry winters accelerate this: when indoor humidity drops below 30%, hardwood contracts and the finish layer develops micro-cracks that let moisture in. Floors that are just starting to show wear are the easiest and cheapest to refinish. Waiting turns a $4/sqft project into a board-replacement job. The estimate is free and takes 20 minutes — it's worth knowing where your floors stand.

How Hardwood Refinishing Works

Step 1 — Inspection: We assess wood species, floor thickness, finish type, and damage depth to give you accurate pricing before anything starts.

Step 2 — Dustless sanding: We sand to bare wood using a HEPA-filtered vacuum system that captures dust at the source — your home stays clean throughout the job.

Step 3 — Stain selection: If you're changing color, we apply test swatches directly on your floor in your actual lighting. You approve the color before we commit to the full room.

Step 4 — Finish coats: Three coats of commercial-grade polyurethane, buffed between each coat. Water-based options available for faster dry times and near-zero odor.

Step 5 — Cure and handoff: We leave you a written cure protocol — exact walk-on time, furniture return window, rug placement guidance — specific to your finish type.

What Does Hardwood Refinishing Cost in Elkhorn?

Full refinishing in Elkhorn, NE runs $3.50–$7 per square foot for sand, stain, and three finish coats. A typical 800 sq ft of hardwood comes to roughly $2,800–$5,600. Screen-and-recoat — a surface refresh with no sanding, for floors still in solid structural condition — costs $1.50–$2.50/sqft. What pushes the number up: heavy damage requiring extra sanding passes, custom stain color, difficult areas like closets and stair landings, and wood species that are harder to sand evenly. The estimate is free and produces an exact written price — no ranges at invoice time.

Frequently Asked Questions

My floors are scratched but not terrible — do I really need full refinishing?

Not necessarily. If the finish is intact and damage is only in the top layer, a screen-and-recoat at $1.50–$2.50/sqft may be enough. We'll tell you honestly during the estimate whether full sanding is warranted. We don't upsell full refinishing when a recoat will do the job.

How long does refinishing take?

A typical project runs 3–4 days from first sanding pass to final coat. Water-based finishes allow sock-foot traffic in 24 hours and furniture return at 72 hours. Oil-based finishes need 48 hours before foot traffic and up to 7 days before heavy furniture moves back in.

Do I need to move out?

Not usually. With our dustless sanding system, most families stay home. The main inconvenience is keeping out of the rooms being worked on. Oil-based finishes have stronger fumes — families with young children or chemical sensitivities sometimes prefer to stay elsewhere for one night. Water-based finishes are nearly odorless.

Will repairs blend with the rest of the floor?

Full refinishing blends repairs seamlessly — everything sands to the same level and takes stain uniformly. If you only refinish part of a floor, some visible difference is possible depending on wood age and species. We flag this before work starts so there are no surprises at the end.
