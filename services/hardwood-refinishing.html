Hardwood Floor Refinishing in Omaha, NE

Restore your worn, scratched, or outdated hardwood floors to like-new condition with professional refinishing from Elkhorn Hardwood. Dustless sanding, 40+ custom stain colors, and a 3-year finish warranty on every project in the Omaha metro.

The Refinishing Process: What to Expect

Hardwood floor refinishing is a multi-step process that completely removes the existing finish and surface damage, then rebuilds the floor to a smooth, durable new surface. Here's how we approach every project in Omaha:

Step 1: Sanding

We use our dust-free HEPA-filtered sanding system to sand your floors down to bare wood. This removes old finish, surface scratches, minor gouges, and stain discoloration. Our drum sanders, edge sanders, and detail hand tools work in sequence to ensure a perfectly flat surface without visible machine marks — a common flaw with less experienced contractors. Nebraska's variable humidity (summers can be quite humid, winters dry) means floors may have experienced more expansion and contraction than in drier climates, so careful sanding that follows the wood grain is especially important here.

Step 2: Stain Application

Once sanded, we can apply any of 40+ custom stain colors — or leave the floor natural. We bring stain samples to your home and apply test patches directly on your floor so you can see exactly how each color looks in your actual lighting before committing. Popular choices for Omaha homes include warm walnut, classic golden oak, trending gray washes, and espresso tones. Stain is applied by hand or with a professional floor machine for even coverage, then allowed to dry completely before finishing.

Step 3: Finish Application

We apply multiple coats of commercial-grade polyurethane using professional applicators for a smooth, bubble-free result. You can choose between:

  • Oil-based polyurethane: Richer amber tone, extremely durable, 8+ hours between coats — best for high-traffic areas
  • Waterborne polyurethane: Clear finish that doesn't yellow, faster dry time, lower VOCs — great for light-colored stains and families with pets or allergies
  • Bona Mega ONE (our standard): Commercial-grade waterborne system that combines durability with environmental friendliness — this is what we use on the majority of Omaha refinishing jobs

We apply a minimum of 3 coats with light sanding between coats for maximum adhesion and a flawless surface.

The Dust-Free Advantage

Our HEPA-filtered dustless sanding system captures 99% of wood dust at the source — directly at the sanding drum before it becomes airborne. This means no plastic sheeting hanging through your home, no post-job deep cleaning, and safe air quality for families with asthma, allergies, or young children. Most homeowners can stay in their homes throughout the process, and furniture can return to the floor within 72 hours of the final coat.

Hardwood Refinishing Cost in Omaha

The average cost for hardwood floor refinishing in Omaha, Nebraska is $3 to $8 per square foot. Here's how the tiers break down:

  • $3–$4/sqft: Basic sand and refinish with no stain change, standard oil-based finish
  • $4–$6/sqft: Full sand, stain change, and 3-coat waterborne finish (most common)
  • $6–$8/sqft: Complex projects — multiple species, tight spaces, heavy damage, premium finishes

A typical 800–1,200 sq ft Omaha home runs $2,400–$7,200 for a full refinish. Screen-and-recoat (surface refresh only, no sanding) costs $1–$2.50/sqft and is appropriate for floors still in good structural condition.

Nebraska Climate Considerations

Omaha experiences significant seasonal humidity swings — humid summers (70–80% relative humidity) and dry winters (20–30% with the heat running). These swings cause hardwood to expand in summer and contract in winter, which stresses both the wood and the finish. We use finish systems specifically rated for wide-humidity-range environments, and recommend keeping indoor humidity between 35–55% year-round to protect your investment.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many times can hardwood floors be refinished?

Solid hardwood floors (¾" thick) can typically be refinished 5–8 times over their lifetime, as each refinish removes approximately 1/32" of wood. Engineered hardwood depends on the thickness of the wear layer — floors with a 3mm+ wear layer can be refinished once or twice. We always inspect your floors before quoting to confirm refinishing is possible and how much life remains.

How long does refinishing take and when can I walk on the floors?

A typical Omaha refinishing project takes 3–5 days from sanding through final coat. With waterborne finishes, you can walk on floors in socks after 24 hours. Furniture can return after 72 hours. Wait 5–7 days before replacing area rugs, and 30 days before putting felt pads on furniture. Nebraska's winters can slightly extend dry times; summers with higher humidity may require extra dry time between coats.

Do I need to move out during refinishing?

With our dustless sanding system, most families stay in their homes during the project. The main inconvenience is not being able to use the rooms being worked on. We schedule work room-by-room when possible for multi-room projects. Oil-based finishes do have a strong odor that may require ventilation, so some families prefer to stay elsewhere for 1–2 nights during that stage. Waterborne finishes have very little odor and are a better option for families with sensitivities.
