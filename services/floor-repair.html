Gap Filling

Nebraska's extreme humidity swings â€” from humid summers to dry-furnace winters â€” cause hardwood boards to expand and contract cyclically. Over years, this can create noticeable gaps between boards, especially in solid hardwood floors installed without proper seasonal moisture compensation.

We fill gaps with flexible wood filler blended to match the stain color, or with thin wood slivers (flooring "splines") cut to size for larger gaps. For floors with systemic gapping, we assess whether the gaps will close in summer (which they often do in Nebraska's humid summers) or whether structural correction is needed. Small seasonal gaps are normal and don't require repair; persistent 1/8" or wider gaps in the dry season can be addressed.

Frequently Asked Questions

In most cases, yes â€” especially when we refinish the entire room after the repair.
